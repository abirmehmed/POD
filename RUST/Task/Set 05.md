## ­¤ōś 10 Conceptual Questions
1. **Procedural Macros vs. Declarative Macros:** You know `macro_rules!`, but how do Procedural Macros (`#[derive(...)]`, `#[attribute]`) differ? Explain how they operate on TokenStreams using the `syn` (parsing) and `quote` (generation) crates, and why they are compiled as separate `proc-macro` crates.
2. **The `unsafe` Contract:** When you write an `unsafe` block, the compiler turns off borrow checking and other safety guarantees. What is the "unsafe contract" or "invariants" that *you*, the developer, must manually guarantee to prevent Undefined Behavior (UB)?
3. **Raw Pointers (`*const T` and `*mut T`):** How do raw pointers differ from references (`&T`)? Why are they not guaranteed to be valid, aligned, or non-null, and why must they be dereferenced inside an `unsafe` block?
4. **`Pin` and `Unpin` in Async Rust:** Why does the `Future` trait require `Pin<Box<T>>` in some contexts? Explain what "pinning" means (preventing memory from being moved) and why self-referential structs (common in async state machines) require it to remain safe.
5. **The `Stream` Trait:** How does `Stream` extend the concept of `Future` to represent a sequence of asynchronous values (like an async iterator)? How does it differ from a synchronous `Iterator`, and when would you use `tokio_stream`?
6. **Property-Based Testing:** How does property-based testing (e.g., using the `proptest` or `quickcheck` crates) differ from traditional example-based unit testing? Why is it incredibly powerful for finding edge cases in parsing, sorting, or mathematical logic?
7. **`no_std` Rust:** What does `#![no_std]` actually do? Which parts of the standard library are removed (e.g., heap allocation, OS APIs), and in what environments (Embedded, WebAssembly, OS kernels) is this strictly required?
8. **Safe Abstractions over Unsafe Code:** What is the golden rule of writing safe Rust wrappers around unsafe code? (Hint: The public API must be 100% safe, and all unsafe invariants must be checked or documented *before* the unsafe block is entered).
9. **FFI Code Generation (`bindgen` / `cbindgen`):** Manually writing `extern "C"` blocks is error-prone. How do `bindgen` (C to Rust) and `cbindgen` (Rust to C) automate this process, and what are their limitations with complex C++ features?
10. **Performance Profiling (Flamegraphs):** How do you move beyond `criterion` to understand *where* CPU time is actually spent? Explain how tools like `perf` and `cargo-flamegraph` sample the call stack to visualize hotspots.
